🚀 The Perinatal Pathway Project


We are embarking on a scoping project to better understand the inpatient experience of women in the perinatal period who are admitted to non-MBU wards. This project responds to queries about what happens when women in the perinatal period who are not admitted to an MBU and are instead placed on acute or PICU wards. The aim is to understand how decisions are made, how care is planned and delivered, and what the experience is like for both women and staff.


The project is rooted in appreciative enquiry. Learning from what works well, exploring care experiences, and identifying practical improvements. It includes interviews with women and staff, pathway mapping, case reviews, and a thematic summary of findings to support quality improvement.


We are taking a focused and manageable first step to understand these pathways in more depth. The goal is to create a model for improvement that could be expanded across the Yorkshire & Humber region. This work complements the core case management function by deepening our insight into how the system works and where it can evolve to better meet patient needs.

System Structure

Understanding how the Yorkshire & Humber perinatal mental health system works together is essential to effective case management. The diagram below shows the key organisations and their relationships in supporting women and families across our region.

Yorkshire & Humber Perinatal Provider Collaborative

Strategic group of regional health and social care partners, who oversee the clinical and operational delivery of the Yorkshire and Humber Mother and Baby Unit.

West Yorkshire Commissioning Hub

Provides oversight and coordination for specialist mental health commissioning, manages contracts and funding for perinatal inpatient services and ensures services meet regional needs and national standards.

Y&H Perinatal Case Manager

Point of contact for the Commissioning hub who supports admissions, transfers and discharges across the region alongside the clinical teams, and ensures commissioned packages of care are safe, effective and of high quality.

Y&H Referral Management Service (RMS)

Manages day-to-day referrals for perinatal inpatient services, ensures appropriate triage and supports the admission process to the local MBU or out-of-area (OOA) inpatient services as appropriate.

Y&H Mother and Baby Unit

Provides inpatient care for mothers and their babies, offering a safe and therapeutic environment to support recovery, while working closely with community teams for continuity of care.

Y&H Out of Area Inpatients

RMS maintains clinical oversight of anyone in the perinatal period who is placed OOA and supports relevant repatriations. Case Manager with ensure quality and commissioning of all OOA placements, in liaison with the OOA PCs and ICBs, to ensure Y&H patients receive safe and effective care in-line with the commissioning agreement and contract.

This structure ensures coordinated care across the region, with clear pathways for referrals, admissions, and ongoing support. Each component plays a vital role in delivering high-quality perinatal mental health services to women and families in Yorkshire & Humber.
