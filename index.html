üöÄ The Perinatal Pathway Project

We are embarking on a six-week scoping project in Leeds to better understand the inpatient experience of women in the perinatal period who are admitted to non-MBU wards. This project responds to queries about what happens when women in the perinatal period who are not admitted to an MBU and are instead placed on acute or PICU wards. The aim is to understand how decisions are made, how care is planned and delivered, and what the experience is like for both women and staff.


The project is rooted in appreciative enquiry. Learning from what works well, exploring care experiences, and identifying practical improvements. It includes interviews with women and staff, pathway mapping, case reviews, and a thematic summary of findings to support quality improvement.


By starting in Leeds, we are taking a focused and manageable first step to understand these pathways in more depth. The goal is to create a model for improvement that could be expanded across the Yorkshire & Humber region. This work complements the core case management function by deepening our insight into how the system works and where it can evolve to better meet patient needs.
